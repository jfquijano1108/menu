History

There are several versions about the origin of this cocktail. The oldest known story traces its origin to a 19th century pirate, Puerto Rican Roberto Cofresí, who distributed among the crew of his ship a curious concoction that contained coconut, pineapple and white rum. From this mixture came the recipe for what would later become the famous Piña Colada, which gets its name from the fruit with the one that is made, and the “colada” comes from the coconut juice with which it is mixed. After being executed in 1825, the recipe was lost.


After years this preparation was made with new flavors such as mango
